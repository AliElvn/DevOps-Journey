# 📅 Day 04 - Linux, Distributions and WSL

## 🎯 Goal

Understand what Linux really is and why developers use WSL2.

---

## 📚 What I Learned

- Linux is the kernel, not a complete operating system.
- Ubuntu, Debian, Fedora and Arch are Linux distributions.
- A distribution combines the Linux kernel with system tools, package managers and other software.
- Different distributions exist because they have different goals and philosophies.
- Stability is often more important than having the newest software in production environments.
- WSL2 runs a real Linux kernel inside Windows.
- WSL2 helps developers work in an environment that is much closer to production servers.
- One of the biggest problems in software development is environment differences, often summarized by the phrase: "It works on my machine."

---

## 💻 What I Did

- Learned the difference between Linux and Linux distributions.
- Understood why companies choose stable distributions like Ubuntu Server.
- Explored why production environments avoid unnecessary upgrades.
- Learned why WSL2 is widely used by developers and DevOps engineers.

---

## ❌ Mistakes I Made

- I thought Linux itself was a complete operating system.
- I assumed WSL2 was just a virtual machine.

---

## 💡 Biggest Lesson

Matching the development environment with the production environment reduces unexpected problems.

---

## 🧠 Reflection

Today I learned that Linux is only the kernel.

Distributions build complete operating systems around that kernel, and WSL2 allows me to work with a real Linux environment while staying on Windows.
